United States: Country Profile

The United States is a religiously diverse country with a significant Muslim population. Muslim communities in the U.S. include immigrants and citizens from various ethnic, cultural, and national backgrounds.

According to research, Arab and Muslim communities have experienced increasing social distance and discrimination over time, particularly influenced by media representation and global political events (Cainkar).

These patterns intensified after September 11, 2001, when public discourse and policy began to associate Muslim identity with national security concerns, contributing to Islamophobia in social and political life.
