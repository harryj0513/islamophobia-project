United States: Country Profile

The United States is a religiously diverse country with a significant Muslim population, made up of immigrants and citizens from various ethnic and cultural backgrounds. Despite this diversity, Muslim communities have experienced increasing marginalization over time.

According to Louise Cainkar, the experiences of Arab and Muslim communities in the United States have shifted significantly, with growing “social distance” created by institutions such as media, government policy, and public discourse. These institutions have contributed to discrimination, exclusion, and negative public attitudes toward Muslims.

Cainkar explains that this social distance is not natural but constructed through cultural representations and political processes. Media portrayals, especially since global political conflicts, have played a key role in shaping how Muslims are perceived in American society.

These patterns intensified after September 11, 2001, when Muslim identity became strongly associated with national security concerns. This shift contributed to widespread Islamophobia, influencing both public perception and policy in the United States.

Source: Cainkar, “The Social Construction of Difference and the Arab American Experience.”
