Islamophobia in U.S. Media Representation

Islamophobia in the United States has been shaped through social, political, and media institutions that construct negative representations of Muslims. These representations increased significantly after the events of September 11, 2001, when public discourse began to associate Islam with violence and security threats.

Media representation in the United States plays a central role in constructing Islamophobia by reinforcing stereotypes, shaping public attitudes, and supporting policies that marginalize Muslim communities (Cainkar; CAIR Report).
